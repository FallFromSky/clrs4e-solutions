\exercise
Let's differentiate both sides of equation (A.11):
\begin{align*}
    \sum_{k=0}^\infty k\cdot kx^{k-1} &= \frac{(1-x)^2+2x(1-x)}{(1-x)^4} \\
    &= \frac{(1-x)+2x}{(1-x)^3} \\
    &= \frac{1+x}{(1-x)^3}.
\end{align*}
To get the desired result, multiply all expressions in the above equation by $x$.
