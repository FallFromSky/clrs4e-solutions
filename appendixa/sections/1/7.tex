The asymptotic upper bound:
\begin{align*}
    \sum_{k=1}^n\!\sqrt{k\lg k} &\le \sum_{k=1}^n\!\sqrt{n\lg n} \\
    &= n\cdot\!\sqrt{n\lg n} \\
    &= O(n^{3/2}\lg^{1/2}n).
\end{align*}
For the asymptotic lower bound, assume for convenience that $n$ is even and at least 4.
Then:
\begin{align*}
    \sum_{k=1}^n\!\sqrt{k\lg k} &> \sum_{k=n/2+1}^n\!\sqrt{k\lg k} \\
    &> \sum_{k=n/2+1}^n\!\sqrt{(n/2)\lg(n/2)} \\
    &= (n/2)\!\sqrt{(n/2)(\lg n-1)} \\
    &\ge (n/2)\!\sqrt{(n/2)(\lg n)/2} \\
    &= (n^{3/2}\lg^{1/2}n)/4 \\
    &= \Omega(n^{3/2}\lg^{1/2}n).
\end{align*}
The asymptotically tight bound follows from Theorem 3.1.
