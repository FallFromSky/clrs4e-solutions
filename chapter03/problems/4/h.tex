Let $h(n)=o(f(n))$.
Then, for any positive constant $c$ there exists a positive constant $n_0$ such that $0\le h(n)<cf(n)$ for all $n\ge n_0$.
This implies that
\begin{align*}
    f(n) &\le f(n)+o(f(n)) \\
    &= f(n)+h(n) \\
    &< (c+1)f(n) \\
    &< 2f(n),
\end{align*}
so $f(n)+o(f(n))=\Theta(f(n))$.
