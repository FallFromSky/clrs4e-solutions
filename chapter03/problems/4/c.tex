True.
Suppose that $f(n)=O(g(n))$.
Let $c$ and $n_0$ be positive constants such that $1\le f(n)\le cg(n)$ and $\lg g(n)\ge1$ for all $n\ge n_0$.
Then,
\begin{align*}
    \lg f(n) &\le \lg c+\lg g(n) \\
    &\le \lg c\cdot\lg g(n)+\lg g(n) \\
    &= (\lg c+1)\lg g(n) \\
    &= O(\lg g(n)).
\end{align*}
